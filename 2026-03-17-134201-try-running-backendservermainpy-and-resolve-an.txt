● Good question. Here's what's happening and what you can do about it:

  Why age appears weak

  1. CCI/Elixhauser absorb the age signal — older patients have more
  comorbidities, so cci_score and elixhauser_score already carry much of what
  age would contribute. The tree-based models (LGBM, RF) see age as redundant
  once comorbidity scores are present.
  2. Linear ordinal encoding (0–9) is flat — it assumes each decade adds equal
  risk, but readmission risk is non-linear (spikes at 70+ and especially 80+).
  3. The keep list drops individual comorbidity flags —
  add_comorbidities_to_dataframe computes ~45 binary flags
  (cci_congestive_heart_failure, elix_renal_failure, etc.) but they're all
  filtered out. The aggregate scores mask which specific conditions actually
  drive readmission.

  Concrete suggestions

  High impact — features you already have but aren't using:

  1. Include individual comorbidity flags — CHF, renal failure, COPD, and
  depression are clinically the strongest readmission predictors. Add at least
  the high-weight ones to keep instead of relying only on aggregate scores.
  2. Include A1Cresult, max_glu_serum, diabetesMed, change — these are in the
  UCI dataset but currently dropped. Medication changes during hospitalization
  and poor glycemic control are strong diabetes-specific readmission signals.

  Medium impact — new engineered features:

  3. Age-adjusted CCI — the original Charlson index adds 1 point per decade over
   40. Your comorbidipy score doesn't include this:
  df["age_adjusted_cci"] = df["cci_score"] + np.maximum(0, df["age"] - 4)
  4. Age × utilization interactions (frailty proxies) — without a formal frailty
   instrument, these approximate it:
  df["age_x_inpatient"] = df["age"] * df["number_inpatient"]
  df["age_x_medications"] = df["age"] * df["num_medications"]  # polypharmacy in
   elderly
  df["age_x_cci"] = df["age"] * df["cci_score"]
  5. Non-linear age thresholds:
  df["age_over_70"] = (df["age"] >= 7).astype(int)
  df["age_over_80"] = (df["age"] >= 8).astype(int)

  Lower impact but worth trying:

  6. Discharge-to-care-facility + high comorbidity — patients discharged to SNF
  with high CCI almost certainly get readmitted. An interaction feature here
  could help.

  The single biggest win is probably #1 and #2 — you're computing the
  comorbidity flags but throwing them away, and ignoring diabetes-specific
  clinical columns that the dataset provides. Want me to implement any of these?
